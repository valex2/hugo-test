About me

I’m currently a hardware engineer at Until, working on biological time travel.

I believe that the future of humanity is sweetly nestled in the intersection of biology and electrical engineering. I believe that continued progress in hardware innovation will enable at-scale distribution of advances in BCIs, health sensing/treatments, and biomanufacturing – technologies that will improve lives at a scale of n » 1,000,000.

I’m particularly interested in developing systems that leverage progress across EE and Biology. I’m also interested in developing coherent technology policy that leaves room for R&D, public buy-in, and accessibility for all.

In my free time, I love spearfishing, playing spikeball, and thinking about things that make me feel small: the brain, the ocean, and the stars.
